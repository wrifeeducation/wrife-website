# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

## What PWP Is

**Progressive Writing Practice (PWP) is an independent daily application system** where pupils practice sentence construction formulas learned in main lessons through:

- **AI-powered scaffolding** (primary delivery mechanism)
- **Systematic sentence evolution** (building complexity incrementally)
- **Self-directed learning** (pupils work independently, 5-20 minutes)
- **Adaptive progression** (formulas match individual mastery levels)
- **Paragraph composition** (begins at multi-clause sentence stage)

## What PWP Is NOT

PWP is **not**:
- A separate grammar teaching session
- Teacher-led instruction during the session
- Random sentence practice
- Disconnected from the main WriFe curriculum
- Traditional "grammar exercises"

## The Core Principle

**Main Lessons (L1-67) teach grammatical concepts in authentic writing contexts.**  
**PWP applies those concepts through independent systematic practice the next day.**

**Result:** Grammar integrated with writing, not taught separately.

## 2. The PWP Session Structure

### Stage 1: Foundation Stage (L10-15)

**Duration:** 5 minutes  
**Formulas:** 2-4 per session  
**Paragraph writing:** None yet (single-clause sentences only)

**Example - L11 Session:**
```
Subject given: "Maria"

Formula 1: subject + verb
‚Üí Pupil writes: "Maria dances"

Formula 2: subject + verb + object
‚Üí Pupil writes: "Maria dances tango"

Formula 3: determiner + subject + verb
‚Üí Pupil writes: "The Maria dances"

Formula 4: determiner + subject + verb + determiner + object
‚Üí Pupil writes: "The Maria dances the tango"

Review phase: AI provides Socratic feedback on any errors
Session complete
```

---

### Stage 2: Development Stage (L16-30)

**Duration:** 10-12 minutes  
**Formulas:** 5-7 per session  
**Paragraph writing:** BEGINS (simple before/after prompts)

**Example - L23 Session:**
```
Subject chosen by pupil: "Ben"

Formulas 1-7: Systematic evolution
1. Ben walks
2. Ben walks slowly  
3. Ben walks slowly home
4. The tired Ben walks slowly home
5. Yesterday, the tired Ben walks slowly home [pupil error - reviewed later]
6. Yesterday, the tired Ben walked slowly home [corrected]
7. Yesterday, after school, the tired Ben walked slowly home

Review phase: AI asks Socratic questions about Formula 5 tense error

Paragraph writing:
Final sentence: "Yesterday, after school, the tired Ben walked slowly home."

AI generates contextual questions:
ü§î Think about your story:
‚Ä¢ Where was Ben before he started walking home?
‚Ä¢ Why was Ben tired?
‚Ä¢ What did Ben do when he arrived home?

Pupil writes 4-5 sentence paragraph

Session complete
```

---

### Stage 3: Application Stage (L30-50)

**Duration:** 12-15 minutes  
**Formulas:** 8-10 per session  
**Paragraph writing:** Standard practice with richer prompts

**Subject conditions:** "Choose a place" or "Not a person today"

**Paragraph prompts evolve:**
- "What do you see, hear, and feel in this moment?"
- "Describe the setting in detail"
- "Show the character's emotions through actions"

---

### Stage 4: Advanced Stage (L50-67)

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes  
**Formulas:** 10+ per session  
**Paragraph writing:** Thematic/perspective conditions

**Paragraph prompts:**
- "Write from the villain's perspective"
- "Theme: Hero's return"
- "Theme: Rags to riches"
- "Show this moment from a different character's viewpoint"

**Culmination:** Pupils can apply sentence construction skills to ANY writing genre - teacher just shows format/conventions.

## 3. Systematic Formula Evolution

### The Core Mechanism

**Each formula builds on the previous by adding ONE element.**

**NOT random separate sentences.**  
**NOT different subjects each formula.**  
**Systematic transformation of ONE foundation sentence.**

### Example Evolution Sequence (L25):

```
Foundation: "Dog runs"

Formula 1: subject + verb
‚Üí Pupil writes complete sentence: "Dog runs"

Formula 2: subject + adverb + verb  
‚Üí Pupil REWRITES complete sentence: "Dog quickly runs"
   (Wrote "Dog" again, wrote "runs" again, added "quickly")

Formula 3: subject + adverb + verb + preposition phrase
‚Üí Pupil REWRITES complete sentence: "Dog quickly runs through park"
   (Wrote "Dog" 3rd time, "runs" 3rd time, "quickly" 2nd time, added phrase)

Formula 4: determiner + adjective + subject + adverb + verb + prep phrase
‚Üí Pupil REWRITES complete sentence: "The energetic dog quickly runs through park"
   (Wrote "dog" 4th time, "runs" 4th time, added "the energetic")

Formula 5: det + adj + subject + adverb + verb + prep + det + adj + object
‚Üí Pupil REWRITES complete sentence: "The energetic dog quickly runs through the muddy park"
   (Wrote "dog" 5th time, "runs" 5th time, enhanced "park")

Formula 6: fronted adverbial + det + adj + subject + adverb + verb + prep phrase
‚Üí Pupil REWRITES complete sentence: "Every morning, the energetic dog quickly runs through the muddy park"
   (Wrote "dog" 6th time, "runs" 6th time, all other words again)

[Continue to 8-10 formulas depending on stage]
```

**CRITICAL: Pupils REWRITE the entire sentence each time, not just "add bits."**

**Each step is recognizable as evolution of "Dog runs" - the foundation remains visible.**

---

### The Repetition Principle

**Why complete rewriting matters:**

**Traditional "gap-filling" approach:**
```
Formula 2: [Dog] [_____] [runs]  ‚Üê Just fill the gap
```
- Minimal repetition
- Focus on new element only
- Weak memory formation
- No motor memory development

**WriFe complete rewriting approach:**
```
Formula 2: Write entire new sentence: "Dog quickly runs"
```
- Full repetition of previous elements
- Integration of new element with known elements
- Strong memory formation through motor practice
- Neural pathway reinforcement

**By Formula 6, pupils have written "Dog" SIX times and "runs" SIX times.**

This is not busywork - this is **systematic spaced repetition built into the structure.**

### Pedagogical Basis

**Neuroscience of learning:**

1. **Spaced Repetition Effect** (Ebbinghaus)
   - Information repeated over intervals (formulas 1-7) retained longer
   - Each formula = spaced repetition session
   - Built into structure, no separate revision needed

2. **Motor Learning** (Schmidt)
   - Physical act of writing reinforces memory
   - Hand-brain connection strengthens neural pathways
   - Each rewriting = motor practice session

3. **Deliberate Practice** (Ericsson)
   - Focused attention on ONE new element each time
   - Repetition of mastered elements reduces cognitive load
   - Systematic skill building through incremental complexity

4. **Retrieval Practice** (Bjork)
   - Each formula requires recall of previous elements
   - Active retrieval stronger than passive review
   - Testing effect built into every formula

**Result:** By Formula 7-10, foundation sentence is AUTOMATIC. Pupils don't think about "dog runs" - they KNOW it.

## 4. AI-Powered Scaffolding System

### Primary Delivery Mechanism

**PWP is delivered primarily through digital AI system because:**

1. **Adaptive formula generation** - AI generates formulas based on:
   - Curriculum map (what lesson teaches)
   - Individual mastery data (what pupil has mastered)
   - Performance patterns (what needs reinforcement)

2. **Socratic feedback** - AI teaches through questions, not answers

3. **Contextual paragraph prompts** - AI generates questions specific to pupil's sentence

4. **Automatic remediation** - AI detects struggles and provides micro-sessions

5. **Infinite variation** - AI can vary subjects, tenses, contexts to prevent memorization

### AI Formula Generation Algorithm

```python
def generate_pwp_formulas(pupil_id, lesson_number):
    """
    Generate systematic formula progression for individual pupil
    """
    # Step 1: Get curriculum baseline
    baseline_concepts = curriculum_map[lesson_number]
    # Example L23: [noun, verb, determiner, adjective, adverb, preposition, fronted_adverbial]
    
    # Step 2: Check pupil's individual mastery
    mastery_data = get_mastery_status(pupil_id)
    # Returns: {noun: MASTERED, verb: MASTERED, determiner: MASTERED,
    #          adjective: MASTERED, adverb: PRACTICING, 
    #          preposition: NEEDS_SUPPORT, fronted_adverbial: NEW}
    
    # Step 3: Determine formula count based on stage
    if lesson_number <= 15:
        formula_count = 3-4
    elif lesson_number <= 30:
        formula_count = 5-7
    elif lesson_number <= 50:
        formula_count = 8-10
    else:
        formula_count = 10-12
    
    # Step 4: Generate systematic progression
    formulas = []
    foundation = "subject + verb"  # Always start here
    formulas.append(foundation)
    
    # Build progressively
    # Formulas 1-4: Use MASTERED concepts (confidence building)
    # Formulas 5-7: Include PRACTICING concepts (consolidation)
    # Formulas 8-10: Challenge with NEW/NEEDS_SUPPORT concepts (growth)
    
    for i in range(formula_count - 1):
        previous_formula = formulas[-1]
        
        if i < 3:  # Early formulas - use mastered concepts
            next_element = choose_from(mastery_data, status="MASTERED")
        elif i < 6:  # Middle formulas - include practicing
            next_element = choose_from(mastery_data, status="PRACTICING")
        else:  # Later formulas - push boundaries
            next_element = choose_from(mastery_data, status=["NEW", "NEEDS_SUPPORT"])
        
        new_formula = add_element(previous_formula, next_element)
        formulas.append(new_formula)
    
    return formulas
```

### Mastery Criteria

**AI considers concept "mastered" using weighted factors:**

**PRIMARY (60% weight): Time since introduction**
```
10+ lessons ago + used regularly ‚Üí MASTERED
3-9 lessons ago ‚Üí PRACTICING
0-2 lessons ago ‚Üí NEW/EMERGING
```

**SECONDARY (30% weight): Accuracy percentage**
```
80%+ ‚Üí Strong
60-79% ‚Üí Developing  
<60% ‚Üí Needs support
```

**TERTIARY (10% weight): Consistency trend**
```
Recent improvement ‚Üí Confidence building
Recent decline ‚Üí May need review
```

## 6. Subject Selection Progression

### Phase 1: Subject Given (First ~5 PWP sessions)

**Teacher/AI assigns subject:**
```
L10 PWP: Subject = "Dog"
L11 PWP: Subject = "Maria"  
L12 PWP: Subject = "Ben"
L13 PWP: Subject = "Bird"
L14 PWP: Subject = "Car"
```

**Purpose:** Pupils focus on learning the PWP routine and understanding formulas without the cognitive load of choosing subjects.

---

### Phase 2: Free Choice (After ~5 sessions)

**Pupil chooses own subject:**
```
L15 PWP: "Choose your subject - any person, animal, place, or thing"

Pupil chooses: "London"

All formulas use "London" throughout session
```

**Purpose:** Growing independence while maintaining focus.

---

### Phase 3: Conditional Choice (Ongoing)

**Conditions prevent defaulting to familiar subjects:**

**Early conditions (L20-35):**
- "Choose a place" (forces abstract thinking about non-animate subjects)
- "Not a person today" (prevents always writing about people)
- "Choose an animal"
- "Choose a thing" (most challenging)

**Middle conditions (L35-50):**
- "Choose a place and use past tense"
- "Choose a thing and describe it moving"
- "Write about weather"

**Advanced conditions (L50-67):**
- "Write from a villain's perspective"
- "Theme: Rags to riches"
- "Theme: Hero's return"
- "Write as if recounting a childhood memory"

**Purpose:** 
- Prevent pupils from always choosing comfortable subjects (people)
- Force application of formulas across different contexts
- Develop flexible writing skills
- Prepare for thematic/perspective work

```
- Collaborative writing activities

- AI scaffolds, teacher monitors
- No instruction during PWP

- Grammar taught in writing context (not isolation)
- Immediate application through PWP
- Distributed practice throughout week
- Skills integrated, not separated
```

**Traditional Approach (Separated):**
```
Grammar Lesson ‚Üí Decontextualized exercises ‚Üí Hope for transfer

Problem: Skills don't transfer to authentic writing
```

**WriFe Approach (Integrated):**
```
Concept taught in context ‚Üí Immediate application ‚Üí Distributed practice ‚Üí Automatic use

Result: Skills embedded in authentic writing
```

# CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

## 1. Maintaining Independence

**Threat:** Teachers revert to instruction during PWP

**Prevention:**
- Clear training on teacher role
- Dashboard designed for monitoring, not intervention
- Time expectations (2-5 min monitoring, not 20 min teaching)
- Celebration of pupil independence

---

## 2. AI Feedback Quality

**Threat:** AI gives answers instead of questions

**Prevention:**
- Socratic prompt engineering
- Regular review of AI responses
- Teacher override capability
- Continuous improvement based on examples

---

## 3. Physical Booklet Self-Sufficiency

**Threat:** Booklet pupils dependent on teacher help

**Prevention:**
- Extensive worked examples
- Clear self-check mechanisms
- Tiered difficulty with clear stopping points
- Teacher training on minimal intervention

---

## 4. Preventing PWP-Main Lesson Disconnect

**Threat:** PWP becomes "just an exercise" unconnected to real writing

**Prevention:**
- Explicit teaching of connection in training
- Dashboard shows correlation metrics
- Teacher references PWP growth in main lessons
- Celebration of sentence evolution in authentic writing

---

## 5. Sustaining Teacher Buy-In

**Threat:** Teachers see dashboard monitoring as "extra work"

**Prevention:**
- 2-5 minute daily time commitment (realistic)
- Data actually useful for planning (not busywork)
- Visible pupil progress (motivating)
- Reduced marking burden (AI handles PWP feedback)
