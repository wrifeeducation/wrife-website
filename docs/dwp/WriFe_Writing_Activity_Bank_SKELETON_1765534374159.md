# WriFe Daily Writing Practice (DWP)
## Progressive Writing Activity Bank - Skeleton Outline

**Purpose:** Bank of writing activities that pupils complete in the "Your Writing" text box, progressing from word sorting through to short narrative composition.

**Integration:** Complements the Interactive Practice Activities (Duolingo-style) with actual writing production.

**Assessment:** AI-powered feedback using Claude API with age-appropriate, encouraging guidance.

---

## PROGRESSION OVERVIEW

**40 Progressive Levels Across 8 Tiers**

| Tier | Focus | Levels | Complexity |
|------|-------|--------|------------|
| 1 | Word Awareness | 1-5 | Sorting and identifying |
| 2 | Word Combinations | 6-10 | Pairing words logically |
| 3 | Simple Sentences | 11-17 | Basic sentence construction |
| 4 | Sentence Expansion | 18-24 | Adding detail and clauses |
| 5 | Basic Sequencing | 25-28 | Connecting sentences |
| 6 | BME Structure | 29-33 | Beginning-Middle-End framework |
| 7 | Enhanced Sentences | 34-37 | Complex sentences in context |
| 8 | Short Narratives | 38-40 | Complete story writing |

## TIER 1: WORD AWARENESS (Levels 1-5)
**Objective:** Recognize and categorize words by type

### LEVEL 1: Sort Your Story Words
**Activity Type:** Word sorting into categories
**Writing Task:** "Copy these words into three groups: PEOPLE, PLACES, THINGS"
**Word Bank:** 10 words from pupil's personal story (grandmother, park, football, teacher, London, bicycle, doctor, school, book, friend)
**Success Criteria:** 8/10 words sorted correctly
**AI Assessment Focus:** Category understanding, ability to follow instructions

**Example Prompt:**
```
Copy these words into three groups:

Words: grandmother, park, football, teacher, London, bicycle, doctor, school, book, friend

PEOPLE:
[pupil writes here]

PLACES:
[pupil writes here]

THINGS:
[pupil writes here]
```

---

### LEVEL 2: Find the Action Words
**Activity Type:** Identifying and writing verbs
**Writing Task:** "Write the action word from each sentence"
**Given:** 5 simple sentences
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 verbs identified correctly
**AI Assessment Focus:** Verb recognition, spelling accuracy

**Example Prompt:**
```
Read each sentence. Write ONLY the action word (what someone is doing).

1. The girl jumps over the puddle.
   Action word: _____________

2. My brother reads every night.
   Action word: _____________

3. Birds sing in the morning.
   Action word: _____________
```

---

### LEVEL 3: Write Word Pairs
**Activity Type:** Creating noun + verb combinations
**Writing Task:** "Match each person/thing with an action they can do"
**Word Banks:** Nouns (5) | Verbs (5)
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 logical pairs
**AI Assessment Focus:** Logical thinking, grammar understanding

**Example Prompt:**
```
Match each word from Column A with an action from Column B.
Write your pairs below.

Column A (Who/What):    Column B (Action):
birds                   swim
children                bark
dogs                    sing
fish                    teach
teachers                play

Write your pairs:
1. _______________ + _______________
2. _______________ + _______________
[etc.]
```

---

### LEVEL 4: Capital Letters for Names
**Activity Type:** Rewriting with proper capitalization
**Writing Task:** "Copy these names with the correct capital letters"
**Given:** 10 words (mix of common and proper nouns)
**Success Criteria:** 8/10 correctly capitalized
**AI Assessment Focus:** Proper noun understanding, attention to detail

---

### LEVEL 5: Complete Word Sort Challenge
**Activity Type:** Four-category sorting (TIER 1 FINALE)
**Writing Task:** "Sort 10 words into PEOPLE, PLACES, THINGS, or ACTIONS"
**Word Bank:** Mixed word classes
**Success Criteria:** 8/10 correct (unlocks TIER 2)
**AI Assessment Focus:** Comprehensive word class knowledge

## TIER 2: WORD COMBINATIONS (Levels 6-10)
**Objective:** Construct meaningful word pairs and triplets

### LEVEL 6: Add a Noun
**Activity Type:** Complete phrases with appropriate nouns
**Writing Task:** "Add a person, place, or thing to complete each phrase"
**Given:** 5 incomplete phrases (e.g., "the big ___", "my happy ___")
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 logical completions
**AI Assessment Focus:** Context understanding, vocabulary

**Example Prompt:**
```
Complete each phrase by adding a PERSON, PLACE, or THING:

1. the big ___________
2. my happy ___________
3. a small ___________
4. the old ___________
5. our favorite ___________
```

---

### LEVEL 7: Add an Action
**Activity Type:** Complete phrases with appropriate verbs
**Writing Task:** "Add an action word to tell what each person/thing does"
**Given:** 5 subjects (e.g., "My dog ___", "The teacher ___")
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 logical completions

---

### LEVEL 8: Build Three-Word Phrases
**Activity Type:** Create determiner + noun + verb patterns
**Writing Task:** "Write 5 phrases using this pattern: THE/A + PERSON/THING + ACTION"
**Word Banks Provided:** Determiners, Nouns, Verbs
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 grammatically correct phrases

**Example Prompt:**
```
Use words from the boxes to write 5 phrases.
Pattern: THE/A + PERSON/THING + ACTION

Box 1 (THE/A):    Box 2 (Who/What):    Box 3 (Does):
the                dog                   runs
a                  girl                  jumps
                   teacher               reads
                   cat                   sleeps
                   bird                  sings

Write your phrases:
1. _______________________________
2. _______________________________
[etc.]
```

---

### LEVEL 9: Add Describing Words
**Activity Type:** Expand noun phrases with adjectives
**Writing Task:** "Add a describing word before each person/thing"
**Given:** 5 basic noun phrases
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 appropriate adjectives added

---

### LEVEL 10: Word Chain Challenge
**Activity Type:** Build progressively longer phrases (TIER 2 FINALE)
**Writing Task:** "Start with one word, add one word each time"
**Pattern:** dog → big dog → big dog runs → the big dog runs
**Success Criteria:** Complete 5 chains correctly

## TIER 3: SIMPLE SENTENCES (Levels 11-17)
**Objective:** Write complete, grammatically correct simple sentences

### LEVEL 11: Copy Perfect Sentences
**Activity Type:** Sentence copying with correct punctuation
**Writing Task:** "Copy these 5 sentences exactly, with capital letters and full stops"
**Given:** 5 model sentences
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 copied accurately (capitals, full stops, spelling)
**AI Assessment Focus:** Sentence awareness, punctuation, accuracy

---

### LEVEL 12: Fix the Sentences
**Activity Type:** Add missing capitals and full stops
**Writing Task:** "Rewrite these sentences with correct punctuation"
**Given:** 5 sentences without capitals/full stops
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 correctly punctuated

**Example Prompt:**
```
Rewrite these sentences with capital letters and full stops:

1. the dog runs in the park
   _______________________________

2. my sister reads her book
   _______________________________
```

---

### LEVEL 13: Complete the Sentences
**Activity Type:** Finish sentence stems
**Writing Task:** "Finish each sentence by adding an action and full stop"
**Given:** 5 sentence stems (e.g., "My teacher ___")
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 completed logically with correct punctuation

---

### LEVEL 14: Write Your Own Sentences
**Activity Type:** Independent sentence writing (first time!)
**Writing Task:** "Write 3 sentences about yourself using these words"
**Word Bank:** 5 verbs provided (like, play, go, eat, have)
**Success Criteria:** 3/3 complete sentences (subject + verb + full stop)
**AI Assessment Focus:** Sentence structure, creativity, confidence

**Example Prompt:**
```
Write 3 sentences about yourself.
Use one of these action words in each sentence: like, play, go, eat, have

Remember:
- Start with a capital letter
- End with a full stop
- Make sure it's a complete thought

1. _______________________________
2. _______________________________
3. _______________________________
```

---

### LEVEL 15: Add a Where
**Activity Type:** Expand sentences with place information
**Writing Task:** "Add WHERE to each sentence"
**Given:** 5 basic sentences (e.g., "I play football")
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 sentences expanded with appropriate places

---

### LEVEL 16: Add a When
**Activity Type:** Expand sentences with time information
**Writing Task:** "Add WHEN to each sentence"
**Given:** 5 basic sentences
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 sentences expanded with appropriate time words

---

### LEVEL 17: Sentence Building Challenge
**Activity Type:** Write 5 complete sentences with WHO, WHAT, WHERE (TIER 3 FINALE)
**Writing Task:** "Write 5 sentences. Each must tell WHO, WHAT they do, and WHERE"
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 sentences complete with all three elements

---

## TIER 4: SENTENCE EXPANSION (Levels 18-24)
**Objective:** Add detail and variety to sentences using phrases and clauses

### LEVEL 18: Add Adjectives
**Activity Type:** Enhance nouns with describing words
**Writing Task:** "Rewrite 5 sentences, adding at least 2 describing words"
**Given:** 5 basic sentences
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 enhanced with appropriate adjectives

**Example Prompt:**
```
Make these sentences more interesting by adding describing words (adjectives).

Basic: The dog ran.
Better: The big, brown dog ran.

Now you try:
1. The cat sat. 
   _______________________________

2. My friend plays.
   _______________________________
```

---

### LEVEL 19: Use "And" to Join
**Activity Type:** Compound sentences with coordination
**Writing Task:** "Join pairs of sentences using 'and'"
**Given:** 10 short sentences (5 pairs)
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 pairs joined correctly

---

### LEVEL 20: Use "But" to Contrast
**Activity Type:** Compound sentences with contrast
**Writing Task:** "Write 4 sentences using 'but' to show a contrast"
**Starter Provided:** First part of each sentence given
**Success Criteria:** 3/4 logical contrasts

---

### LEVEL 21: Add a "Because"
**Activity Type:** Causal clauses
**Writing Task:** "Add 'because' and a reason to 5 sentences"
**Given:** 5 statement sentences
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 logical reasons added

---

### LEVEL 22: Use "When" for Time
**Activity Type:** Temporal clauses
**Writing Task:** "Write 5 sentences using 'when' to show time"
**Pattern Examples Provided**
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 sentences with correct clause structure

---

### LEVEL 23: Multiple Details
**Activity Type:** Sentences with 2+ additional elements
**Writing Task:** "Write 3 sentences. Each must have WHERE, WHEN, and WHY"
**Success Criteria:** 3/3 sentences with all required elements

---

### LEVEL 24: Formula Sentence Practice
**Activity Type:** Follow specific sentence patterns (TIER 4 FINALE)
**Writing Task:** "Write 5 sentences following these patterns"
**Given:** 5 different formula patterns (e.g., Adjective + Noun + Verb + Where + When)
**Success Criteria:** 4/5 sentences follow patterns correctly

## TIER 5: BASIC SEQUENCING (Levels 25-28)
**Objective:** Connect sentences in logical order

### LEVEL 25: First, Next, Then, Last
**Activity Type:** Sequence 4 sentences with connectives
**Writing Task:** "Write 4 sentences about [given topic] using First, Next, Then, Last"
**Topic:** Simple activity (making a sandwich, getting ready for school)
**Success Criteria:** 4 sentences in logical sequence with connectives

**Example Prompt:**
```
Write 4 sentences about making a sandwich.
Start each sentence with: First, Next, Then, Last

First, _______________________________
Next, _______________________________
Then, _______________________________
Last, _______________________________
```

---

### LEVEL 26: Write 3 Connected Sentences
**Activity Type:** Short sequence without frame
**Writing Task:** "Write 3 sentences that go together about [topic]"
**Topic Options:** Playing a game, walking to school, eating dinner
**Success Criteria:** 3 sentences that connect logically

---

### LEVEL 27: Before and After
**Activity Type:** Temporal sequencing
**Writing Task:** "Write what happens before and after each event"
**Given:** 3 middle events
**Success Criteria:** 6 sentences (2 per event) in logical order

---

### LEVEL 28: Tell What Happened
**Activity Type:** Recount 5 connected sentences (TIER 5 FINALE)
**Writing Task:** "Write 5 sentences about something you did yesterday"
**Success Criteria:** 5 sentences in chronological order, logical flow

## TIER 6: BME STRUCTURE (Levels 29-33)
**Objective:** Write using Beginning-Middle-End story framework

### LEVEL 29: Beginning Sentences
**Activity Type:** Craft effective story openings
**Writing Task:** "Write 3 different beginning sentences for these stories"
**Given:** 3 story titles/topics
**Success Criteria:** 3 openings that introduce character/setting

**Example Prompt:**
```
Write a BEGINNING sentence for each story.
Your sentence should tell WHO and WHERE.

Story 1: The Lost Ball
Beginning: _______________________________

Story 2: A Surprise Visit
Beginning: _______________________________

Story 3: The Storm
Beginning: _______________________________
```

---

### LEVEL 30: Middle Sentences (The Problem)
**Activity Type:** Develop story conflict/action
**Writing Task:** "Write 2 sentences for the MIDDLE of each story"
**Given:** 3 story beginnings
**Success Criteria:** 6 sentences (2 per story) that develop action/problem

---

### LEVEL 31: Ending Sentences (The Solution)
**Activity Type:** Craft effective resolutions
**Writing Task:** "Write an ending sentence for each story"
**Given:** 3 story beginning-middles
**Success Criteria:** 3 endings that resolve the story

---

### LEVEL 32: Complete BME Plan
**Activity Type:** Plan all three parts
**Writing Task:** "Plan a story with Beginning, Middle, and End"
**Prompt:** Story about "A Special Day"
**Success Criteria:** 1 sentence for B, 2 for M, 1 for E (4 total, logical flow)

**Example Prompt:**
```
Plan your story "A Special Day"

BEGINNING (WHO and WHERE):
_______________________________

MIDDLE (WHAT HAPPENED - write 2 sentences):
1. _______________________________
2. _______________________________

ENDING (HOW IT FINISHED):
_______________________________
```

---

### LEVEL 33: Write Your First Story
**Activity Type:** Complete 5-sentence story (TIER 6 FINALE)
**Writing Task:** "Write a short story with Beginning, Middle, End"
**Topic Choice:** 3 options provided
**Success Criteria:** 5+ sentences, clear BME structure, logical flow
**AI Assessment Focus:** Story structure, coherence, creativity

## TIER 8: SHORT NARRATIVES (Levels 38-40)
**Objective:** Write complete short narratives with enhanced sentences

### LEVEL 38: Story with Detailed Beginning
**Activity Type:** Narrative with expanded opening
**Writing Task:** "Write a 7-sentence story. Use 3 sentences for the beginning"
**Topic:** Personal experience or imagination
**Success Criteria:** 7 sentences, 3-sentence opening with setting/character details, clear BME

**Example Prompt:**
```
Write a short story about "An Unexpected Friend"

Remember:
- BEGINNING: 3 sentences (WHO, WHERE, WHEN, introduce the situation)
- MIDDLE: 3 sentences (WHAT HAPPENED, the problem or action)
- ENDING: 1 sentence (HOW IT FINISHED)

Use interesting describing words and different sentence starters.

[Writing space]
```

---

### LEVEL 39: Story with Enhanced Details
**Activity Type:** Narrative with sentence variety
**Writing Task:** "Write an 8-sentence story using at least 3 different sentence starters"
**Topic Choice:** 3 options
**Success Criteria:** 8+ sentences, 3+ different starters, clear BME, good flow

---

### LEVEL 40: Your Best Short Narrative
**Activity Type:** Final showcase story (TIER 8 FINALE / PROGRAMME COMPLETION)
**Writing Task:** "Write your best short story (10-12 sentences)"
**Requirements:**
  - Clear BME structure
  - At least 2 complex sentences
  - Describing words (adjectives)
  - Different sentence starters
  - Interesting vocabulary
**Success Criteria:** 10+ sentences, meets all requirements, engaging and coherent
**AI Assessment Focus:** Comprehensive writing ability, creativity, technical skill
**Completion:** Award "WriFe Writing Master" certificate

## ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK

### AI Assessment Categories (All Levels)

**1. Task Completion (30%)**
- Did pupil complete the required number of items?
- Did they follow the instructions?

**2. Technical Accuracy (30%)**
- Spelling (age-appropriate expectations)
- Punctuation (capitals, full stops, commas where taught)
- Grammar (subject-verb agreement, tense consistency)

**3. Content Quality (25%)**
- Does the writing make sense?
- Is it logical and coherent?
- Is vocabulary appropriate?

**4. Structural Understanding (15%)**
- Does pupil demonstrate understanding of the concept being taught?
- Are patterns followed correctly?
- Is sentence/story structure appropriate?

### Feedback Structure (All Levels)

**Main Message:**
- Acknowledges effort and completion
- States what they did well

**Specific Praise (1-2 things):**
- Technical success (punctuation, spelling)
- Creative choice (good word choice, interesting idea)

**Growth Area (1 thing only):**
- ONE specific area to improve
- Teaching guidance, not just correction

**Next Step:**
- Clear guidance on what to practice
- Encouragement to continue

**Encouragement:**
- Positive closing
- Celebration of progress

## INTEGRATION WITH DUOLINGO-STYLE ACTIVITIES

### Two-Part Daily Practice Model

**PART 1: Interactive Practice (5 mins)**
- Duolingo-style activities (already built)
- Click, drag, select, sort
- Immediate right/wrong feedback
- Gamified progression

**PART 2: Writing Practice (5-10 mins)**
- Open text box activities (this bank)
- Actual writing production
- AI assessment and feedback
- Skill application

### Synchronization

**Same Level, Two Components:**
- Level 1 Interactive: Sort words by clicking buttons
- Level 1 Writing: Copy words into written groups

**Benefits:**
- Reinforcement through different modalities
- Knowledge → Application
- Recognition → Production
- Interactive practice scaffolds writing practice
